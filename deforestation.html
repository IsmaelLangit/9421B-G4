What is Deforestation?

It is removing or clearing large areas of forest lands so that we could use it for our advantage knowing that there would be a bad impact to the environment.

Ground survery mission by Greenpeace.

The looting of Borneo didn’t start with palm oil, and it doesn’t end there either. Humans have had a long and complicated history with the island. Chinese traders traveled to Borneo for rhinocerous horn as well as timber, National Geographic reports. Muslim and Portuguese traders were interested in the island’s pepper and gold. In the 19th and 20th centuries, loggers began to flood Borneo. Europe, the United States and Australia have been drilling for oil and natural gases in Borneo for decades. Coal minining is also currently a threat, as it pollutes local rivers. Read more...

Before and After: Devastating Deforestation in Photos By Nicole Bonaccorso

In the 1950s, two alien machines were introduced to the forests of Borneo, changing the island forever: the chainsaw and the caterpillar tractor. Since then, illegal logging has become all too familiar for those in Borneo, causing devastating damage to one of the most biologically diverse habitats on Earth. According to the World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF), between 1985 and 2001, illegal logging decimated 56 percent of lowland tropical rainforest in the Indonesian territory of Kalimantan. That equates to more than 18,000 square miles of rainforest. Read more...
